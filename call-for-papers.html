Call for Papers

We invite submissions on how foundation models generalize beyond their training distributions and how they can be adapted efficiently, continually, and reliably after deployment. We welcome theoretical and empirical work, datasets, benchmarks, and case studies, and explicitly encourage negative results, ablations, and analyses of failure modes.

Topics of Interest

  • Foundations of generalization: emergent, in-context, domain, and compositional generalization; theory and mechanistic understanding; the role of pretraining data; calibration and robustness under distribution shifts.
  • Adaptation mechanisms: parameter-efficient fine-tuning, model merging and composition, test-time adaptation, inference-time scaling, in-context and retrieval-augmented adaptation, continual learning, and agentic adaptation via reasoning, tool use, memory, and RL.
  • Reliability and safety: uncertainty estimation, calibration, abstention, self-assessment, and failure detection under shift and evolving environments.
  • Applications: robotics, scientific discovery, time-series analysis, biomedicine, and remote sensing.
  • Evaluation: contamination-resistant and continually evolving benchmarks, robust evaluation of agentic systems, and reproducible empirical validation.

Submission Guidelines

  • Two tracks: short papers (up to 4 pages) and long papers (up to 9 pages), both excluding references and appendices.
  • Submissions must use the NeurIPS 2026 workshop format and will be reviewed double-blind.
  • All submissions are managed through OpenReview. Each paper receives at least three reviews.
  • The workshop is non-archival: accepted papers do not appear in formal proceedings and authors remain free to submit elsewhere. With author consent, accepted papers will be linked from this website.
  • We do not accept work already published at an ML or related venue, nor work appearing at the main NeurIPS 2026 conference. Concurrent submission to other venues is permitted and must be disclosed at submission time.

Submission portal: OpenReview link (to be released with the call for papers).
